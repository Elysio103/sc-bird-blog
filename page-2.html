Types of Birds

Tufted Titmouse

Scientific name: Baeolophus bicolor
Length: 5.5-6.3 in
Weight: 0.6-0.9 oz
Wingspan: 7.9-10.2 in

Common around feeders and in backyards. They are defined by their small crests that resemble mohawks. Generally a gray color with a lighter color below, almost resembling a mouse with wings. Can be seen year-round in South Carolina.

Brown Thrasher

Scientific name: Toxostoma rufum
Length: 9.1-11.8 in
Weight: 2.1-3.1 oz
Wingspan: 11.4-12.6 in

Characterized by their warm brown color with a streaked belly and breast. They have also have yellow eyes and a black beak. Thrashers are also songbirds
with a wide range of songs. You'll spot them eating in dry leaves on the ground, looking for insects.

Carolina Wren

Scientific name: Thryothorus ludovicianus
Length: 4.7-5.5 in
Weight: 0.6-0.8 oz
Wingspan: 11.4 in

You'll spot them by their reddish-brown top and lighter-orange bottom colors. They also have slightly curved beaks and white eyebrows. They tend to be hard to find as they're good hiders, making them hard to find by bird watchers, though they have a certain song they sing that might help. This bird is also South Carolina's state bird.

Brown-headed Nuthatch

Scientific name: Sitta pusilla
Length: 3.9-4.3 in
Weight: 0.3oz
Wingspan: 6.3-7.1 in

These nuthatches have a black chiseled beak, a bluish gray back, cream white chest, and a brown head. They're more found in pine forests, so if you have any near you, you'll be able to spot them, generally around the trunks to eat. They have a squeaking noise that resembles a rubber duck.

American Oystercatcher

Scientific name: Haematopus palliatus
Length: 15.8-17.3 in
Weight: 14.1-24.7 oz
Wingspan: 31-35 in

Easy to spot from their red-yellow eyes, these birds are found around the shorelines, nesting and searching for shellfish such as clams and oysters.
Though, not always successful if their prey clamps on their bills. Another name these birds used to go by is called a "sea pie".

Brown Pelican

Scientific name: Pelecanus occidentalis
Length:39.4-53.9
Weight: 70.5-176.4 oz
Wingspan: 78.7 in

A rather large seabird, these pelicans can be spotted on beaches and the ocean. They are gray-brown with a yellow head and white neck. Their eating behavior consists of diving into the water, scooping up any fish they catch, and draining any excess water. The male will help bring nest materials for the female to lay eggs.
